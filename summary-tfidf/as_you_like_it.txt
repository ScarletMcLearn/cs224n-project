Enter and Patience once more, whiles our compact is urged: You say, if bring in your Rosalind, You will bestow her on Orlando here? Orlando doth commend him to you both, And to that youth he calls his Rosalind He sends this bloody napkin. Will you, Orlando, have to wife this Rosalind? There is a man haunts the forest, that abuses our young plants with carving 'Rosalind' on their barks; hangs odes upon hawthorns and elegies on brambles, all, forsooth, deifying the name of Rosalind: if could meet that fancy-monger would give him some good counsel, for he seems to have the quotidian of love upon him.
